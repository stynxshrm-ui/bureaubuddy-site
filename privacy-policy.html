Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Short version: BureauBuddy reads document text on supported pages only when you click the button. We do not store, sell, or share your document content. Text is sent to an AI model to generate a summary and immediately discarded.

What we collect

BureauBuddy collects the minimum data necessary to function:

  • Document text — extracted from the page only when you click "Explain this letter". This text is sent to our backend and then to an AI model (Mistral AI) to generate a summary. It is not stored.
  • Anonymous device ID — a randomly generated UUID stored locally in your browser. Used only to enforce the free tier limit (5 analyses per month). It is not linked to your identity.
  • Usage count — how many analyses you have performed this month, stored locally in your browser.

What we do NOT collect

  • Your name, email address, or any personal identity information
  • Your Gmail login credentials or email content beyond what you explicitly analyze
  • Browsing history or activity outside of supported sites
  • Any data when you are not actively using the extension

How your data is used

When you click "Explain this letter":

  • Text is extracted from the visible document on the current page
  • This text is sent to our backend server (hosted on Vercel)
  • Our backend forwards the text to Mistral AI's API to generate a plain-English summary
  • The summary is returned to your browser and displayed in the side panel
  • The document text is not logged or stored at any point

Third-party services

BureauBuddy uses the following third-party services:

  • Mistral AI — processes document text to generate summaries. Subject to Mistral's privacy policy.
  • Vercel — hosts our backend API. Subject to Vercel's privacy policy.

Data storage

All data stored by BureauBuddy lives in your browser's local storage (chrome.storage.local). This includes your anonymous device ID and usage count. This data never leaves your device except as described above.

Permissions

BureauBuddy requests the following Chrome permissions:

  • activeTab — to read document text on the current tab when you click the button
  • storage — to save your device ID and usage count locally
  • scripting — to inject the explain button and side panel into supported pages
  • Host permissions for mail.google.com, kivra.se, and minmyndighetspost.se — to operate on these specific sites only

Children's privacy

BureauBuddy is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the product evolves. The "last updated" date at the top will reflect any changes. Continued use of the extension constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
